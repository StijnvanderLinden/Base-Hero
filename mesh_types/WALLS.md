# Walls

## Gameplay Purpose

Walls protect the base, shape enemy paths, and create readable defensive boundaries. They must be instantly recognizable as sturdy obstacles.

## Visual Identity

- Modular and tileable
- Chunky and reinforced
- Engineered rather than natural
- Built from wood, stone, metal bands, braces, or energy anchors

## Shape Language

- Wide, thick silhouettes
- Strong vertical posts
- Horizontal planks, blocks, or panels
- Reinforced corners
- Clear damage-resistant feel

## Required Components

- Main wall body
- Left/right connection points or posts
- Reinforcement bands or braces
- Ground contact/base
- Optional blue energy node for upgraded variants

## Do

- Make modules align cleanly side by side.
- Keep the silhouette simple and sturdy.
- Use readable reinforcement shapes.
- Make upgraded walls visually stronger through larger forms, not tiny details.
- Keep pivot at bottom center.

## Don't

- Use thin fences as main defensive walls.
- Add excessive tiny nails or bolts.
- Make shapes too realistic or medieval-generic.
- Leave gaps between modular pieces.
- Use high-poly rubble or complex cracks.

## Variation Ideas

- Wood plank wall
- Stone block wall
- Wood and brass reinforced wall
- Energy-braced wall
- Heavy corner post
- Gate-like reinforced segment

## Example Roles

- Basic wall: cheap, simple, wooden
- Reinforced wall: thicker, stone/metal braced
- Energy wall: blue core anchors, stronger visual tech identity
