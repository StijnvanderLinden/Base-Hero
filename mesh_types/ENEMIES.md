# Enemies

## Gameplay Purpose

Enemies create pressure against the base and player. They must be readable by role, threat level, and movement type.

## Visual Identity

- Mech-like, not organic
- Darker and more hostile than player/base tech
- Built from crude mechanical forms
- Angular, aggressive silhouettes
- Clear weak/strong role differences

## Shape Language

- Distinct body profiles by role
- Clear head/core area
- Chunky limbs or mechanical supports
- Threat accents in red, orange, or harsh metal
- Avoid organic creature anatomy unless mechanically abstracted

## Required Components

- Main body/core
- Movement supports or legs
- Role-defining weapon or feature
- Threat accent
- Clear front-facing direction

## Do

- Make role readable by silhouette.
- Exaggerate proportions by enemy type.
- Use darker materials than player/base defenses.
- Keep hitboxes visually honest.
- Use larger forms for higher threat.

## Don't

- Make enemies look like animals or generic monsters.
- Use tiny weapon details to define role.
- Create overly complex robot designs.
- Make all enemies the same body with color swaps only.
- Use sleek futuristic robot styling.

## Role Guidelines

### Scout

- Small and fast
- Thin but still readable
- Light frame
- Minimal armor

### Grunt

- Standard body size
- Balanced silhouette
- Simple attack shape
- Easy to read in groups

### Bruiser

- Large torso
- Heavy limbs
- Reinforced armor plates
- Slow, high-pressure silhouette

### Ranged

- Visible launcher, arm cannon, or emitter
- Slightly back-heavy or raised weapon shape
- Clear firing direction

### Boss

- Oversized silhouette
- Multiple readable components
- Large core or weapon feature
- Strong stage presence without excessive detail

## Variation Ideas

- Scrap walker
- Stone-plate automaton
- Core-powered crawler
- Launcher mech
- Shielded bruiser
- Siege boss
