# Era 1: Stone Age Specification

## Purpose
Stone Age is the first fully playable era slice.

It teaches the gate loop through:
- primitive materials
- short pylon defense runs
- simple weapon upgrades
- simple structure upgrades
- a small crystal-gated research set

---

## Theme
Stone Age is primitive, survival-focused, and intentionally low complexity.

Visual direction:
- wood
- stone
- rope
- bone

The era should feel readable and grounded rather than magical or advanced.

---

## Enemy Set
Stone Age uses one self-contained enemy family.

### Caveman
- melee pressure unit
- low health
- medium movement speed

### Brute
- heavy melee pressure unit
- high health
- slow movement
- high damage

### Beast
- medium health
- charge-based pressure spike
- breaks routine in mixed waves

### Stone Mech
- final-wave mini boss
- large silhouette
- slow movement
- very high health
- high structure pressure

---

## Wave Plan
Stone Age pylon channeling uses four short waves.

Wave 1:
- cavemen only

Wave 2:
- cavemen and brutes

Wave 3:
- mixed cavemen, brutes, and beasts

Final Wave:
- stone mech with support pressure

The intended session length is short and readable for the first gate slice.

---

## Player Combat
Stone Age uses one basic weapon profile:
- Stone Staff

Current attacks:
- basic attack on left click
- slightly stronger heavy attack on right click

Starter augment direction:
- damage
- attack speed
- range
- optional small AoE pulse

Stone Age intentionally avoids complex magical behavior trees or layered augment interactions.

---

## Structures
Stone Age structure play stays simple.

### Wooden Wall
- first wall variant
- low health
- cheap stone and wood cost

### Reinforced Wall
- unlocked wall upgrade
- higher health
- higher material cost

### Thrower Turret
- first turret variant
- throws slow heavy projectiles
- simple support role

### Improved Thrower
- first turret upgrade
- better health, damage, and cadence

Spike Trap is deferred for now.

---

## Materials
Stone Age uses:
- Stone
- Wood
- Herbs as a low-priority placeholder

Crystals remain a separate universal research resource.

---

## Pylon And Crystal Integration
Stone Age keeps one pylon rule set:
- one pylon may be placed
- channeling consumes raw stone and wood
- channeling generates essence
- channeling expands influence radius
- pylon influence reports nearby crystals without revealing exact positions

Crystals are intentionally easy to find in this era and are used sparingly.

---

## Progression Slice
Stone Age progression should feel fast.

Current unlock targets:
- Reinforced Wall
- Improved Thrower
- first augment slot
- first branch unlock
- first simple augment nodes

Costs use:
- essence
- a small crystal count

The era should teach progression choice without overwhelming the player.
